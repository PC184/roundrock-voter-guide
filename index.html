What to know before you go

🪪 Bring valid photo ID

Texas requires a government-issued photo ID: TX driver's license, TX ID card, U.S. passport, military ID, or TX handgun license. IDs expired up to 4 years are accepted. No photo ID? Call 1-800-252-VOTE for alternatives.

📝 Your ballot will be long — prepare ahead

The November general election ballot includes dozens of races: Governor, U.S. Senate, your U.S. House district, state legislature, Railroad Commissioner, county offices, and more. Print your sample ballot from BallotReady or Ballotpedia and bring notes. Cell phones are not permitted within 100 feet of voting stations, but written notes are allowed.

📬 Voting by mail — new ID requirements

Texas now requires you include your Driver's License/Texas ID number or the last four digits of your Social Security number on your mail ballot application and ballot. Include both to avoid rejection. Track your mail ballot status at the Texas Secretary of State's ballot tracker.

♿ Need curbside voting?

All Williamson County polling locations offer curbside voting for those who cannot enter the building. Look for posted signs at the entrance — poll workers will come to your car.

🌐 Need language assistance?

Help is available: English/Spanish: 844-TX-VOTES  |  Asian languages: 888-API-VOTE  |  Arabic: 844-YALLA-US  |  ASL video: 301-818-VOTE

Get more involved in Round Rock

Voting is just the beginning. Here are concrete ways to shape your community this election season and beyond.

🗳️ Apply to be a poll worker for November 3

Williamson County pays poll workers for Election Day. General elections need more workers than primaries. Contact the Williamson County Elections Office at 301 SE Inner Loop, Suite 104, Georgetown, TX 78626 or visit wilcotx.gov/elections to apply before October.

📢 Tell 5 neighbors about October 5

The voter registration deadline of October 5 is the most time-sensitive action right now. Personally remind 5 people you know to check their registration at VoteTexas.gov before that date. A text message takes 30 seconds.

🚗 Organize rides to the polls

Transportation is one of the biggest barriers to voting. Coordinate with neighbors, friends, or your precinct chair to organize rides during early voting (Oct 19–30) or on Election Day (Nov 3). Even one extra voter per trip adds up across a precinct.

🎤 Speak at Round Rock City Council

Round Rock City Council meets at City Hall, 221 E. Main St. All meetings include public comment periods. Check roundrocktexas.gov for the current meeting schedule.

🏛️ Attend a Williamson County Commissioners Court meeting

The Commissioners Court meets at 710 Main St, Georgetown and at 1801 E. Old Settlers Blvd, Round Rock. Decisions made here — including the county tax rate and budget — are made in these open public meetings. You have the right to comment.

🤝 Connect with your precinct chair

Your local precinct chair connects your neighborhood to the political process. They can link you to voter registration drives, candidate info, volunteer opportunities, and rides to the polls for the November election.

📋 Host a voter registration table before October 5

Set up a simple table with printed registration applications at a community event, farmers market, or local business before the October 5 deadline. Forms are free from any library or at sos.state.tx.us. Bring a QR code to VoteTexas.gov for on-the-spot digital registration.
