What to know before you go

🪪 Bring valid photo ID

Texas requires a government-issued photo ID: TX driver's license, TX ID card, U.S. passport, military ID, or TX handgun license. IDs expired up to 4 years are accepted. Questions? Call 1-800-252-VOTE.

📝 You can bring a written cheat sheet

Texas allows voters to bring written or printed notes into the voting booth. Print your sample ballot from BallotReady or Ballotpedia before you go. Cell phones are not permitted within 100 feet of voting stations.

♿ Need curbside voting?

All Williamson County polling locations offer curbside voting for those who cannot enter the building. Look for posted signs at the entrance with a text number — poll workers will come to your car.

🌐 Need language assistance?

Help is available by phone: English/Spanish: 844-TX-VOTES  |  Asian languages: 888-API-VOTE  |  Arabic: 844-YALLA-US  |  ASL video: 301-818-VOTE

Get more involved in Round Rock

Voting is just the beginning. Here are concrete ways to shape your community — pick whatever feels right for where you are right now.

🗳️ Apply to be a poll worker

Williamson County pays poll workers for Election Day. Contact the Williamson County Elections Office at 301 SE Inner Loop, Suite 104, Georgetown, TX 78626 or visit wilcotx.gov/elections to apply. Round Rock polling locations include the Baca Center, Wilco Jester Annex, and others.

🎤 Speak at Round Rock City Council

Round Rock City Council meets at City Hall, 221 E. Main St. Meetings include public comment periods — any resident can address the council directly. Check roundrocktexas.gov for the current meeting schedule.

🏛️ Attend a Williamson County Commissioners Court meeting

The Commissioners Court meets at 710 Main St, Georgetown and at 1801 E. Old Settlers Blvd, Round Rock. Meetings are public with open comment periods. Decisions here affect your property taxes, roads, health services, and county budget.

🏫 Attend a Round Rock ISD board meeting

RRISD Board of Trustees meets at the Lillie Delgado Administration Building, 1311 Round Rock Ave. The board is making key decisions on budget, curriculum, and trustee vacancies. All meetings are open to the public.

🤝 Connect with your precinct chair

Your local precinct chair is the most direct link between your neighborhood and the political process. They can connect you to voter registration drives, local candidate information, and volunteer opportunities right in your precinct.

📢 Tell 5 neighbors about the next election

The single most effective action any resident can take: personally tell 5 people you know about an upcoming election, where to vote, and why local races matter. Round Rock City Council and Williamson County races are often decided by very small margins.

📋 Register voters at local events

Texas voter registration is free and easy to do in person. Bring printed applications (available at any library or from sos.state.tx.us) and a QR code linking to VoteTexas.gov to community events, farmers markets, and neighborhood gatherings.
